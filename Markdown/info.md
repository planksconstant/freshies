### A Few Things to Remember

- ⏰ **Be on time.** While some faculty members may allow late entry occasionally, attendance after the class has begun is entirely at their discretion.
- 🚪 The **first class of the day** generally has a short grace period of about 5–10 minutes. This flexibility usually **does not apply after breaks**, so make sure you're back before the next session starts.
- 📅 Depending on your timetable, certain days may have fewer classes, lab sessions, or early finishes.
- 🎉 **Wednesdays** are often scheduled as **half-days** for many programmes, although this can vary by department and semester.

The 2 schedules will be followed as below:

1. **Schedule A** is followed by B.Tech, BBA LLB, BCA, MCA, Design, and Psychology, and a few other branches during the **odd semester**.
2. **Schedule B** is followed by the remaining branches during the odd semester.

> **Note:** The schedules followed are swapped between the odd and even semesters. So if your classes begin at 8 in the odd semester, they will start at 8:45 in the even semester and vice versa.

### 🪪 Carry Your ID Card

Your **PESU ID card is compulsory** for entering the campus and accessing various facilities. Forgot it? In some cases, showing your profile on the PESU App may work, but that's entirely at the discretion of the security staff. To avoid unnecessary delays (or awkward conversations), it's best to keep your ID card with you at all times.

### 📚 Attendance Matters

To be eligible for your **End Semester Assessments (ESAs)**, you must maintain **at least 75% attendance in every course**. Attendance is calculated separately for each subject, so it's important to keep track of it throughout the semester.

In exceptional situations — such as medical emergencies or other genuine, well-documented reasons — the university may consider relaxation on a case-by-case basis. However, these are exceptions rather than the norm. As a general rule, the **75% attendance requirement is enforced strictly**, so it's always best to attend classes regularly rather than rely on the possibility of an exemption.

# Examinations

Examinations at PES University are divided into two components: **In-Semester Assessments (ISAs)** and **End Semester Assessments (ESAs)**. Together, they determine your final grade for each course.

ISAs are conducted twice every semester and are designed to evaluate your understanding of the course as it progresses.

## 📝 In-Semester Assessments (ISAs)

**Key Details**

- **Number of Exams:** 2 ISAs per subject every semester.
- **Duration:** 1 hour 5 minutes.
- **Portions:** 1st ISA — Unit 1 and Unit 2; 2nd ISA — Unit 3 and Unit 4.
- **Marks:** Each ISA is conducted for 40 marks. Together, the ISAs contribute 50% of your final course grade.
- **Venue:** University examination centres (The JAGS Nest in RRC and Mechanical Block in ECC).

### Exam Pattern

The question paper is **completely digital** and is displayed on the **computer provided at your examination desk**. There are **no printed question papers**.

The paper generally consists of a combination of:

- **Descriptive Questions:** 4 questions, each carrying 4 marks. Each unit will have 2 questions.
- **Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs):**
  - 16 questions carrying 1 mark each.
  - 4 questions carrying 2 marks each.

Good news: there is **no negative marking** for MCQs, so it's always worth attempting every question.

For descriptive answers, you'll be provided with a physical answer booklet by the invigilators. Depending on the course, you may type your answers on the computer, write them in the booklet, or use a combination of both.

## End Semester Assessments (ESAs)

ESAs are the comprehensive examinations conducted at the end of each semester.

**Key Details**

- **Duration:** 3 hours
- **Marks:** 100 marks
- **Weightage:** 50% of the final course grade
- **Portions:** All 4 units
- **Question Type:** Entirely descriptive

Similar to ISAs, the question paper is displayed digitally on the computer, while descriptive answers are written in the provided answer booklet unless the course permits typing. In many cases, students can also use a combination of both methods — for example, typing explanations while drawing diagrams in the answer booklet.

## The Answer Booklet

The front page of your answer booklet contains an **OMR sheet**, which is scanned before evaluation. You must carefully fill in details such as:

- SRN
- Name in capital letters ONLY
- Signature below the name in the same box
- Subject Code — will be displayed on the screen
- Subject Name — Full name (e.g. ~~Chemistry~~ ❌ → Engineering Chemistry ✅)
- Examination Slot (Morning/Evening)
- Any other required examination details

> ⚠️ **Fill the OMR sheet carefully.** Since the booklet is digitized immediately after the examination, errors on the OMR sheet may lead to complications during evaluation. Double-check all entries before submitting your booklet. The question number has to be bubbled on every single page to facilitate easy scanning — questions may be missed and may not be evaluated if not bubbled correctly. The answer booklet consists of 20 pages for ISAs and 40 pages for ESAs, and it already has graphs or log sheets and space for rough working as well, eliminating the need for extra answer sheets or tying anything. Extra booklets aren't provided.

### Before the Exam

- 📅 The examination timetable is usually released about a week in advance.
- 🪑 Your seating arrangement is available on the PESU App or PESU Academy Portal, usually on the night before or the morning of the examination. It changes for every exam.

### How Question Papers Are Prepared

One of the unique aspects of PESU examinations is that the **final question paper is generated only on the day of the examination**.

Here's how it works:

- The **Anchor Faculty** and **Co-Anchor Faculty** each prepare a separate question paper.
- On the day of the examination, the university's software automatically selects portions from both papers to create the final question paper.
- Since the final paper is generated electronically just before the examination, **no one knows the complete question paper beforehand**, making paper leaks virtually impossible.

### 📄 How Your Papers Are Evaluated

After the examination, every answer booklet is **scanned and digitized**. The evaluation process is entirely digital.

**Digital Evaluation**

Unlike traditional evaluation, an examiner does **not** receive an entire student's answer script. Instead:

- The software assigns one specific question to a faculty member.
- That faculty member evaluates that single question for every student enrolled in the course.
- Which question is assigned to which evaluator is decided by the Anchor Faculty through the evaluation software.

This ensures greater consistency, as the same examiner evaluates the same question across all answer scripts.

### 📑 Evaluation Policy

PES University follows a structured marking scheme for every question. Faculty members evaluate answers based on an official **answer key** that includes:

- Essential technical concepts
- Mandatory keywords
- Important formulae and derivations (where applicable)

You're **not expected to reproduce the answer word-for-word**. Writing in your own words is perfectly acceptable, provided the technical terminology, core concepts, and required keywords are present.

### 🤖 AI-Assisted Evaluation

To improve consistency during evaluation, PES University also employs an **AI-assisted evaluation system**.

Here's how it works:

- The AI software independently evaluates your digitized answers.
- Faculty members simultaneously evaluate the same answers.
- The AI-generated scores serve as a reference to assist the evaluator.
- The faculty member reviews both evaluations, and **the teacher's decision is always final**.

The AI is intended to support the evaluation process by improving consistency and identifying possible discrepancies — it does not replace human judgement.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** Reach the examination centre well before the reporting time. Bengaluru traffic can be unpredictable, so it's always better to arrive early than risk missing your exam.

# Credits & GPA

One of the first academic terms you'll hear at PESU is **credits**. Every course you complete carries a certain number of credits, and these credits, along with your grades, determine your academic progress and GPA.

## 🎓 Understanding the Credit System

Every course offered at PES University is assigned a credit value, depending on the number of lectures, tutorials, and lab sessions it involves.

**Graduation Requirement**

To successfully complete your undergraduate programme, you'll need to earn **160 credits**.

**Course Credits**

Most courses are assigned one of the following credit values: **5, 4, and 2**.

Generally, lab courses carry higher credits, while other subjects may have lower credit values.

### How Do You Earn Credits?

You earn the entire credit value of a course only if you pass it by securing a grade between **S and E**.

For example:

- A 4-credit course with a grade of A → You earn 4 credits.
- A 2-credit course with a grade of C → You earn 2 credits.

### What Happens if You Fail?

If you receive an F grade, you do not earn any credits for that course.

There is **no concept of partial credits** — it's an all-or-nothing system. You'll need to clear the course in a future semester to earn those credits.

> 💡 **Note:** While the total graduation requirement is 160 credits, the exact credit distribution across semesters may vary depending on your programme and curriculum.

## 📊 How Your GPA is Calculated

Your **Grade Point Average (GPA)** reflects your academic performance in a semester. Every course contributes to your GPA based on both the marks obtained and the credits assigned to that course.

**Marks Distribution**

Each course is evaluated using both internal and external assessments.

| Component | Weightage |
|-----------|-----------|
| Internal Assessment | 50 Marks |
| End Semester Assessment (ESA) | 50 Marks |
| **Total** | **100 Marks** |

**Internal Assessment (50 Marks)**

The internal component is made up of:

| Component | Marks |
|-----------|-------|
| ISA 1 | 20 |
| ISA 2 | 20 |
| Assignments / Continuous Assessment | 10 |
| **Total** | **50** |

### 🧪 Lab-Integrated Courses

Some subjects at PES University are lab-integrated courses, where both the theory and laboratory components contribute to your final grade.

**Marks Distribution**

| Component | Marks |
|-----------|-------|
| ISA + Assignments (Internal) | 50 |
| ESA (Theory) | 100 |
| Laboratory Component | 20 |
| **Total Raw Marks** | **120** |

**External Assessment (50 Marks)**

The ESA is conducted for 100 marks, but the score is scaled down to 50 marks before being combined with your internal marks.

Therefore:

```
Final Marks = Internal (50) + Scaled ESA (50) = 100 Marks
```

The final marks determine the letter grade, which is then converted into grade points.

### 🏅 Letter Grades

| Grade | Meaning | Marks | Grade Point |
|-------|---------|-------|-------------|
| S | Outstanding | 90–100 | 10 |
| A | Excellent | 80–89 | 9 |
| B | Very Good | 70–79 | 8 |
| C | Good | 60–69 | 7 |
| D | Fair | 50–59 | 6 |
| E | Satisfactory | 40–49 | 5 |
| F | Fail | 0–39 | 4 |
| W | Withdrawn | — | 0 |
| AP | Audit Pass | 40–100 | 0 |
| AF | Audit Fail | 0–39 | 0 |

> **Note:** Although an **F** grade is associated with a grade point value in the grading table, it is considered a failed course. It does not earn any credits and negatively impacts your GPA until the course is successfully cleared.

**Worked Examples**

Let's take an example of how the grade is calculated for both lab and non-lab courses:

**1. Subject without lab:** ISA = 35/40, ESA = 82/100, Assignment = 10/10

```
ISA + (ESA / 2) + Assignment = 35 + 41 + 10 = 86
```

**2. Subject with lab:** ISA = 35/40, ESA = 82/100, Assignment = 10/10, Lab = 18/20

```
Final Score = ((ESA + Lab) / 120 × 50) + ISA + Assignments
Final Score = ((82 + 18) / 120 × 50) + 35 + 10 = 86.67
```

## 📘 SGPA (Semester Grade Point Average)

Your SGPA represents your performance in a single semester. It is calculated by taking the weighted average of the grade points obtained in all the courses during that semester.

**Formula**

```
SGPA = Σ(Course Credits × Grade Points) / ΣCredits in the Semester
```

**Example**

| Course | Credits | Grade | Grade Point |
|--------|---------|-------|-------------|
| Python | 5 | A | 9 |
| Physics | 5 | S | 10 |
| EEE | 4 | B | 8 |
| Mathematics | 4 | A | 9 |
| Mechanical | 4 | B | 8 |
| EVS | 2 | S | 10 |

```
Total Credit Points = (5×9) + (5×10) + (4×8) + (4×9) + (4×8) + (2×10) = 215
Total Credits       = 5 + 5 + 4 + 4 + 4 + 2 = 24
SGPA                = 215 ÷ 24 = 8.96
```

## 📈 CGPA (Cumulative Grade Point Average)

While SGPA measures your performance in one semester, **CGPA** reflects your overall academic performance across all completed semesters.

It is calculated by considering the credits and grade points of every course you've successfully completed.

**Formula**

```
CGPA = Σ(Credits × Grade Points) / ΣCredits Earned
```

Another simple trick to calculate your CGPA would be to add your SGPA across all semesters and take its average. As you progress through your degree, your CGPA is updated after every semester and serves as the primary indicator of your overall academic performance.

> 💡 **Tip:** Since courses carry different credit values, performing well in higher-credit courses has a greater impact on your SGPA and CGPA than lower-credit courses.

# Understanding Subject Codes

Every subject at PES University follows a structured subject code. Once you know what each part represents, it's easy to identify details about the course.

**Generic Format:**

```
UE jy sb y c n A/B
```

Where:

- `UE` → Undergraduate Engineering
- `jy` → Year of Admission (e.g. 26 = 2026 Batch)
- `sb` → Subject Code (e.g. CS, EC, ME, CY, PH, MA)
